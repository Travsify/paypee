# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Counter-Financing of Terrorism (CFT) Policy

**Entity:** Pickpadi Global Inclusive Limited  
**Version:** 3.2 (April 2026)  
**Classification:** Internal/Compliance

## 1. Introduction
This policy outlines the commitment of Pickpadi Global Inclusive Limited to detect and prevent money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit financial activities. Paypee complies with the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) AML/CFT Regulations 2022 and international FATF recommendations.

## 2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
We implement a tiered KYC (Know Your Customer) framework:
*   **Tier 0 (Unverified):** No transaction capability.
*   **Tier 1 (Individual):** Verified Email, Phone, and NIN. Basic transaction limits apply.
*   **Tier 2 (Business):** Full CAC verification, Director ID checks, and Proof of Address. Enhanced limits.
*   **Biometric Matching:** Mandatory for all users interacting with cross-border crypto/liquidity features via Bitnob/Quidax.

## 3. Sanctions Screening
Every user and transaction is screened in real-time against:
*   UN Sanctions Lists
*   OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control)
*   本地 (Local) PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) databases.
Any match results in an immediate freeze and manual compliance review.

## 4. Transaction Monitoring (KYT)
We utilize automated systems to identify suspicious patterns:
*   **Velocity Checks:** Monitoring for rapid incoming and outgoing transfers.
*   **Structuring:** Detecting efforts to bypass reporting thresholds through multiple small transactions.
*   **High-Risk Jurisdictions:** Enhanced monitoring for transfers to/from countries identified as high-risk by FATF.

## 5. Reporting Obligations
*   **Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR):** Filed with the NFIU within 24 hours of identifying a non-mitigated suspicious activity.
*   **Currency Transaction Reports (CTR):** Filed for transactions exceeding the statutory regulatory thresholds.

## 6. AML Training
All Paypee employees undergo bi-annual AML/CFT awareness training to ensure they can identify red flags and follow internal reporting procedures.

## 7. Record Keeping
All customer identification data and transaction records are maintained for a minimum of 5 years following the termination of the business relationship.
