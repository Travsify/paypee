# Clarification on Onboarding & Service Delivery

**Topic:** Absence of Publicly Accessible Website  
**Date:** April 2026

## 1. Current Operational Status
Paypee is currently in a **Closed Alpha / Private Beta** phase of development. Our platform is strictly controlled to ensure compliance and security during the initial rollout. This approach is intentional and serves several strategic purposes:

## 2. Onboarding Methodology
Given the absence of a public web landing page for sign-ups, we onboard users via:

1.  **Direct Invitation (The "Staged Rollout"):** We issue unique, non-indexed invitation links to pre-vetted business partners and individual beta testers. This allows us to manage server load and compliance monitoring personnel effectively.
2.  **API-First Integration:** Many of our users are **Developers** who interact with Paypee as a headless infrastructure. They onboard via a private Developer Portal where documentation and API keys are issued after manual business vetting.
3.  **B2B Distribution:** We partner with established corporate entities who offer Paypee services to their employees or customers as an embedded financial solution. In these cases, onboarding is handled within the partner's secure environment.

## 3. Service Delivery Mechanism
Service delivery is primarily managed through two channels:

*   **The Paypee Dashboard (Private URL):** A robust, web-based React dashboard (the current workspace environment) accessible only to authenticated and authorized users.
*   **Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS):** We deliver our services (FX, Cards, Payouts) via a RESTful API layer. Our customers integrate these APIs into their own apps, meaning the end-user interaction happens on the *partner's* platform, powered by Paypee's engine.

## 4. Path to Public Release
Paypee plans to launch a public-facing website and marketing portal in **Q3 2026**, following the successful completion of our current regulatory "Sandbox" phase with our banking and liquidity partners. Until then, we maintain a "Stealth Mode" posture to protect our intellectual property and ensure a bug-free experience for our initial cohort of high-value users.
